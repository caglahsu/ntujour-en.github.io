Introduction

About the Graduate Institute of Journalism, National Taiwan University

The Graduate Institute of Journalism at National Taiwan University was established in 1991 to cultivate advanced journalism talent and elevate professional standards in Taiwan's media landscape. Inspired by the world's leading journalism schools yet deeply rooted in Taiwanese society, the institute has grown from admitting just 12 students out of over 600 applicants in its inaugural year into one of NTU's most competitive graduate programs.

The program integrates three streams of resources: working journalists from newspapers, magazines, television, and digital media; cross-campus electives from partner institutions; and the breadth of NTU's academic departments spanning science, engineering, medicine, law, and business. This convergence underpins the institute's signature "learning by doing" philosophy, producing graduates equipped to lead Taiwan's media industry and contribute to global journalism scholarship.

Students may pursue the general journalism track or enroll in the Digital Journalism Specialization Program, with training in data-driven storytelling, social media analysis, and web production. The institute also offers a Bachelor of Social Science pathway for outstanding undergraduates.
